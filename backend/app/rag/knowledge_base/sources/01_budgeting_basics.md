# Budgeting Basics

## What is a Budget?

A budget is a plan for how you spend your money each month. It tells your money where to go instead of wondering where it went.

## The 50/30/20 Rule

A simple budgeting framework:
- **50% Needs** - rent, bills, groceries, transport, loan repayments, health
- **30% Wants** - eating out, entertainment, subscriptions, clothing
- **20% Savings** - emergency fund, savings goals, investments

Example on £2,000/month income:
- Needs: £1,000
- Wants: £600
- Savings: £400

## Needs vs Wants

**Needs** (essential, hard to cut):
- Rent or mortgage
- Gas, electric, water bills
- Groceries
- Transport to work
- Minimum loan/debt repayments
- Medical expenses

**Wants** (discretionary, easier to cut):
- Eating out and takeaways
- Entertainment (cinema, concerts)
- Streaming subscriptions
- Clothing beyond basics
- Gym (if not essential for health)

## How to Track Your Spending

1. **List all income** - salary, freelance, benefits
2. **List all fixed expenses** - same every month (rent, bills)
3. **List all variable expenses** - changes monthly (food, entertainment)
4. **Calculate what's left** - income minus all expenses
5. **Compare to your goals** - are you saving enough?

## Savings Rate

Your savings rate = (income - expenses) / income × 100

- Below 10%: Low - you're living very close to your means
- 10-20%: Average - reasonable but could improve
- 20-30%: Good - building financial resilience
- Above 30%: Excellent - strong financial position

## Common Budgeting Mistakes

- Not accounting for irregular expenses (car repairs, birthdays)
- Forgetting annual subscriptions
- Underestimating food costs
- Not revisiting budget when income or expenses change

## Quick Wins to Save More

- Cancel subscriptions you haven't used in 30 days
- Meal prep to reduce eating out
- Switch to cheaper phone/broadband plan
- Buy own-brand supermarket products
- Walk or cycle instead of driving for short trips
