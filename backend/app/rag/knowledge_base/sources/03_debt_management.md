# Debt Management

## Types of Debt

**Priority debts** (serious consequences if you miss payments):
- Rent or mortgage arrears (risk of eviction or repossession)
- Council tax (risk of bailiffs)
- Energy bills (risk of disconnection)
- Court fines
- TV licence

**Non-priority debts** (still serious, but fewer immediate consequences):
- Credit cards
- Personal loans
- Overdrafts
- Student loans (UK - repaid through payroll only)
- Buy-now-pay-later

## Debt Triage Order (Practical)

1. Keep essentials paid (housing, utilities, food, transport to work)
2. Build/maintain a mini emergency buffer (£500-£1,000)
3. Stop new high-interest debt where possible
4. Choose and execute a payoff strategy consistently

## Debt Payoff Strategies

### Avalanche Method (saves most money)
Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the **highest interest rate** debt first.

Example:
- Credit card: 24% APR -> attack this first
- Personal loan: 12% APR -> attack second
- Car loan: 6% APR -> attack last

**Best for:** people motivated by saving money on interest

### Snowball Method (builds momentum)
Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the **smallest balance** first.

Example:
- Overdraft: £300 -> clear first (quick win)
- Personal loan: £2,000 -> clear second
- Credit card: £5,000 -> clear last

**Best for:** people who need motivation from seeing debts disappear

## Debt-to-Income Ratio

Debt-to-income ratio = monthly debt repayments / monthly income x 100

- Below 20%: Manageable
- 20-35%: Caution - limits savings capacity
- Above 35%: High - seek debt support quickly

## When to Prioritise Debt Over Saving

Pay off high-interest debt (often above 8% APR) before building large investment/savings pots.

Reason:
- if savings return = 4% and debt cost = 20%, net drag is ~16%

Exception:
- keep a small emergency fund while repaying debt, otherwise shocks push you back into borrowing.

## Negotiation and Restructuring Options

- ask lender for temporary payment plan
- request lower APR or hardship arrangement
- consider 0% balance transfer where suitable and affordable
- consolidate only if total cost and behavior risk improve

## Getting Help With Debt (UK)

Free debt advice:
- **StepChange** - stepchange.org
- **National Debtline** - nationaldebtline.org
- **Citizens Advice** - citizensadvice.org.uk
- **MoneyHelper** - moneyhelper.org.uk

Avoid paid debt-advice firms unless you clearly understand fees and alternatives.

## Avoiding Debt Traps

- Avoid payday loans (extremely high effective cost)
- Be cautious with BNPL stacking across multiple merchants
- Avoid credit-card cash advances (fees + immediate interest)
- Turn off unused credit products that trigger impulse spending

## FinLit Connection

If your stress-test job-loss score is weak, debt pressure is often a major cause.

Use this sequence:
1. reduce expensive debt outflow
2. improve monthly surplus
3. re-run stress tests and compare resilience components
