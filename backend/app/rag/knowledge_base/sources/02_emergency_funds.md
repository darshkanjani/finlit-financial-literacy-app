# Emergency Funds

## What is an Emergency Fund?

An emergency fund is money set aside specifically for unexpected costs. It is not for holidays or planned purchases - only for genuine emergencies.

Examples of emergencies:
- Job loss or redundancy
- Unexpected car or home repair
- Medical or dental emergency
- Boiler breaking down
- Urgent travel for family emergency

## How Much Do You Need?

**Standard rule:** 3-6 months of essential expenses

Essential expenses include:
- Rent or mortgage
- Gas, electric, water bills
- Groceries
- Transport (to work)
- Minimum loan repayments
- Basic health costs

**Example:**
If your essential expenses are £1,200/month:
- Minimum fund: £3,600 (3 months)
- Recommended: £7,200 (6 months)

## Adjust for Your Situation

**Save more (6+ months) if you:**
- Have dependants (children, elderly parents)
- Are self-employed or have variable income
- Work in a volatile industry
- Have a health condition that may affect work

**3 months may be enough if you:**
- Have stable full-time employment
- Live with family who can support you
- Have no dependants
- Have other accessible savings

## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund

- **Separate savings account** - not your main account (avoids spending it)
- **Instant access** - you need to withdraw quickly in an emergency
- **Not investments** - investment values can drop exactly when you need the money
- **High-interest easy-access account** - keeps pace with inflation

## How to Build One From Scratch

1. **Set a small first target** - £500 (covers most single emergencies)
2. **Automate it** - set up a standing order on payday
3. **Keep it separate** - open a dedicated savings account
4. **Build gradually** - even £50/month reaches £600 in a year
5. **Celebrate milestones** - 1 month, 3 months, 6 months

## What Happens Without One?

Without an emergency fund you may:
- Go into debt (credit card, overdraft, loans)
- Miss bill payments and damage your credit score
- Be forced to make long-term financial decisions under pressure
- Struggle to cover months of expenses if you lose your job

## Emergency Fund vs Savings Goals

- Emergency fund = defensive (protection against the unexpected)
- Savings goals = offensive (building towards something specific)

Build your emergency fund first before aggressively saving for other goals.
