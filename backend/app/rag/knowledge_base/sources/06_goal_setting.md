# Financial Goal Setting

## Why Set Financial Goals?

Without a goal, saving feels abstract. With a goal, you have:
- A specific target to work toward
- A way to measure progress
- Motivation to keep going

## SMART Financial Goals

Goals should be:
- **Specific** - "Save £3,600 for an emergency fund" not "save more money"
- **Measurable** - exact amount, trackable progress
- **Achievable** - realistic given your current income/expenses
- **Relevant** - aligned with your actual priorities
- **Time-bound** - clear deadline

## Common Financial Goals

### Priority 1: Emergency Fund
- Target: 3-6 months of essential expenses
- Why first: Protects all other goals from being derailed by unexpected costs

### Priority 2: High-Interest Debt Clearance
- Target: Zero balance on debts above 8% APR
- Why second: High interest costs more than savings earn

### Priority 3: Short-Term Goals (1-3 years)
- Holiday, car, home repairs, wedding
- Keep in easy-access savings account

### Priority 4: Medium-Term Goals (3-10 years)
- House deposit, career change fund, further education
- Consider Cash ISA or fixed-term savings

### Priority 5: Long-Term Goals (10+ years)
- Retirement, children's education fund
- Consider pension contributions, Stocks & Shares ISA

## Calculating Required Monthly Savings

Formula: required monthly saving = (target amount - current amount) / months remaining

Example:
- Goal: £5,000 house deposit
- Currently saved: £800
- Months remaining: 18
- Required monthly saving: (£5,000 - £800) / 18 = £233/month

## Prioritising Multiple Goals

If you can't fund all goals at once:
1. Minimum emergency fund (£500-£1,000) first
2. Clear very high-interest debt
3. Build full emergency fund (3-6 months)
4. Then split remaining savings across multiple goals

## Reviewing Goals

Goals should be reviewed when:
- Income changes (pay rise, job loss, new job)
- Major expenses change (new rent, paid off a debt)
- You reach a milestone
- At least every 6 months

## The Role of Automation

The best way to reach goals:
1. Calculate your required monthly saving
2. Set up a standing order for that amount on payday
3. Put it into a dedicated account labelled with the goal
4. Check progress monthly, adjust if needed

Out of sight, out of mind - if you don't see it in your main account, you won't spend it.
